The Great Liberation

Here's what I believe the data points to, and what most commentators get wrong: mass automation by AI is not the end of human purpose — it's the end of forced labor. It is De Grote Bevrijding — the Great Liberation. For the first time in 200,000 years, humanity has the technology to free itself from repetitive, soul-crushing work.

Yes, the transition will be brutal. 22% of jobs disrupted by 2030 is probably an underestimate — I project 40-60% displacement across white-collar and blue-collar sectors by 2030. But the endpoint is abundance. AI doesn't replace the M&A professional — it amplifies them. One AI-augmented analyst can do the work of 5-10 traditional ones. The premium shifts from information gathering to judgment, relationships, and wisdom — the things that make us irreducibly human.

The question for this room: will you be the professionals who ride this wave, or the ones swept away by it?

AI & Trust

The Bullshit Problem

AI doesn't just automate work — it generates arguments more compelling than humans can write. And here's the uncomfortable truth: it does this without a truth function. Philosopher Harry Frankfurt defined "bullshit" as speech produced without concern for truth — not lying (which requires knowing the truth), but speaking with complete indifference to it. By that definition, large language models are the most efficient bullshit machines ever built. They produce fluent, confident, persuasive text that happens to be correct most of the time — but when it's wrong, it's wrong with the same confidence. This is the central paradox of the AI era for professionals who deal in truth and evidence.

AI Is More Persuasive Than Humans

A peer-reviewed study published in Nature Human Behaviour found that GPT-4 with access to personal data had 81.2% higher odds of persuading humans compared to other humans.

  • Personalized GPT-4 won debates 64.4% of the time
  • Effect persisted even when participants knew they were debating AI
  • Preregistered, randomized controlled trial (N = 900)

Salvi et al. (2025), Nature Human Behaviour, 9(8), 1645-1653

The BullshitBench Problem

Can AI detect its own nonsense? Mostly, no. BullshitBench v2 tested 70+ AI models with plausible-sounding but nonsensical prompts — including legal and financial scenarios.

  • Only Claude and Qwen score above 60% on bullshit detection
  • Chain-of-thought reasoning does not help
  • RLHF training exacerbates the problem

BullshitBench v2 & Liang et al. (2025), arXiv

What This Means for M&A — And Why It Matters More Than You Think

AI can produce compelling due diligence reports, persuasive pitchbooks, and sophisticated valuations — but without verification, they may contain confident errors. The role of human judgment in verifying AI output is becoming the single most critical professional skill of the 21st century.

This is the paradox of AI-augmented cognition: AI makes us smarter and dumber simultaneously. When AI handles cognitive work for us, it becomes part of our extended mind — but the brain itself remains unchallenged and undeveloped. The neural pathways that should be strengthened through reasoning and review atrophy when bypassed. The future isn't AI or humans. It's AI plus humans who maintain the discipline to think critically — who use AI to augment their cognition, not replace it.

Your Next Move

What You Should Do — Starting Monday

Knowing the data is not enough. The gap between understanding what's coming and actually preparing for it is where careers are made or broken. Here are concrete, prioritized recommendations for the three professional tracks in this room — based on where the technology is right now, not where it might be in five years.

A Critical Note on Confidentiality & Data Privacy

You work in M&A. You handle non-public, price-sensitive, legally privileged information every day. Do not blindly upload confidential client data to any public AI model.

When I recommend using frontier AI tools below, I mean it — but with professional judgment. Consider these layers:

  • Public consumer tools (ChatGPT, Claude.ai, Gemini) — excellent for learning, non-sensitive research, and building AI fluency. Use anonymized or synthetic data when experimenting.
  • Enterprise/API deployments (Azure OpenAI, AWS Bedrock, Anthropic API) — data is not used for training and contractual protections apply. This is where firm-level adoption should happen.
  • On-premise / private cloud — for the most sensitive work, models can run entirely within your firm's infrastructure. This is where the industry is heading.

The recommendations below are a starting point. You have a professional and ethical responsibility to exercise judgment about what data goes where. Consult your firm's information security policies. If your firm doesn't have an AI usage policy yet — that is itself a red flag.

Why Frontier Models Matter — Even If Your Firm Has Its Own AI

Many of you use internal tools like Hogan Lovells' CRAIG or similar firm-specific AI platforms. These are valuable — but understand what you're working with. CRAIG launched in December 2023 and, like most enterprise legal AI tools, its underlying model likely lags 12-18 months behind the frontier. Enterprise deployments require compliance review, security audits, and integration testing — all of which create delay.

Meanwhile, frontier models like Claude Opus 4.6, GPT-4.5, and Gemini 3.1 Pro (March 2026) are operating at a level that would have been science fiction two years ago — complex legal reasoning, multi-step analysis, nuanced judgment calls across hundreds of pages. The gap between what your firm's tool can do and what the frontier can do is enormous, and it's growing.

This doesn't mean your firm's tools are useless — they're tuned for your specific workflows and data. But if you only interact with enterprise AI, you have no idea what's actually possible. You owe it to yourself — and your clients — to understand the frontier. Use consumer-tier frontier models (with non-sensitive data) to calibrate your understanding of where AI actually is. Then push your firm to close the gap.

For M&A Lawyers & Legal Associates

The majority of this room. Your profession will be unrecognizable within 36 months.

This Week:

  • Get a personal Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus subscription. Start using AI for non-confidential work — research memos, general legal analysis, learning new practice areas. Build the muscle memory. When your firm deploys enterprise AI, you'll be ready to run while others are still learning to walk.
  • Create a synthetic or anonymized version of a recent transaction document and run it through a frontier model. Compare its analysis to yours. The point isn't to use this for client work — it's to understand what the technology can actually do, so you can make informed decisions about adoption.

This Month:

  • Learn prompt engineering for legal contexts. The difference between a bad prompt and a good one is the difference between hallucinated nonsense and partner-quality analysis.
  • Study what Hogan Lovells is doing with ELTEMATE and CRAIG. This is the benchmark. If your firm doesn't have an equivalent, ask why.
  • Map your daily tasks by AI-replaceability. Anything that involves pattern matching, document comparison, or clause extraction has a shelf life of 12-18 months.

This Year:

  • Become the AI person at your firm. The associates who bridge legal expertise and AI fluency will be the partners of 2030. Those who wait will find there are no partnership tracks left.
  • Build expertise in AI governance, algorithmic liability, and autonomous agent regulations — this is where new legal practice areas are being born right now.
  • Prepare for the billable hour's extinction. Firms that charge for outcomes rather than time will dominate. Position yourself for that transition.

For Corporate Finance & Investment Banking

AI is already trading, already modeling, already writing pitch books. The question is whether you're the one directing it.

This Week:

  • Use AI to build a financial model from scratch using public data (earnings reports, market data). Give it raw numbers and see what comes out. Then refine the prompt until the output matches analyst quality. Do this with non-proprietary data first — the goal is to calibrate your understanding of the technology's capabilities.
  • Review your deal pipeline through an AI lens: which targets are most AI-exposed? Which are AI-advantaged? You can do this analysis with publicly available information. This filter will determine winners and losers in every sector.

This Month:

  • Develop an "AI readiness" scoring framework for target companies. Acquirers will pay premiums for AI-native organizations and discount AI-laggards — this is already happening.
  • Study the $4.8 trillion M&A market through the lens of AI-driven consolidation. Industries that resist AI adoption will see forced consolidation as margins compress.
  • Learn to read AI capability benchmarks (MMLU, SWE-bench, ARC-AGI). These are the leading indicators for which industries get disrupted next.

This Year:

  • Build expertise in AI company valuation. Traditional DCF models break down for exponential-growth AI companies. New frameworks are needed and few people understand them yet.
  • Position for the coming wave of AI-driven M&A: distressed acquisitions of AI-laggards, roll-ups of niche AI startups, and strategic AI capability acquisitions by incumbents.
  • Understand that by 2030, AI agents will handle 70% of the analytical work in deal execution. Your value will be in judgment, relationships, and creative deal structuring — invest there.

For Private Equity Professionals

PE is about to experience the most dramatic shift in value creation since the leveraged buyout was invented.

This Week:

  • Audit your portfolio companies for AI readiness. How many have an AI strategy? How many have someone who actually understands what's possible? The gap between their answers and reality is your risk exposure. This is a conversation, not a data upload — no confidential information required.
  • Run a competitive analysis using AI tools with publicly available market data — take a portfolio company's industry and map it against AI disruption timelines. Use the frontier models to understand what level of analysis AI can produce before making enterprise procurement decisions.

This Month:

  • Develop an "AI value creation playbook" for portfolio companies. The fastest path to EBITDA improvement in 2026 is not traditional operational efficiency — it's AI-driven automation of back-office functions.
  • Rethink hold periods. AI is compressing transformation timelines — what used to take 5 years of operational improvement can happen in 18 months with the right AI implementation.
  • Study the ARK Invest convergence thesis: deals at the intersection of AI + robotics + energy storage + blockchain will generate outsized returns.

This Year:

  • Build or acquire AI implementation capability within your fund. The PE firms that can deploy AI across portfolio companies as a standard part of the value creation plan will outperform by 3-5x.
  • Prepare for AI-driven deal sourcing and due diligence. Funds using AI to screen thousands of targets simultaneously will find opportunities that human-only processes miss entirely.
  • The biggest risk in your portfolio isn't leverage or market timing — it's having portfolio companies that are structurally unable to compete in an AI-native economy. Act accordingly.

The Common Thread

Regardless of your track — legal, finance, or PE — the professionals who thrive in the next decade will share three traits: AI fluency (not expertise, fluency — you need to think in AI capabilities the way you currently think in spreadsheets), speed of adaptation (the window between "new technology" and "table stakes" has collapsed from decades to months), and intellectual honesty (the courage to admit that the world you trained for no longer exists). The ones who move first don't just survive. They define the new rules.
