AI & Trust

The Persuasion Problem

AI doesn't just automate work — it generates arguments more compelling than humans can write. This raises fundamental questions about truth, trust, and verification in professional services.

AI Is More Persuasive Than Humans

A peer-reviewed study published in Nature Human Behaviour found that GPT-4 with access to personal data had 81.2% higher odds of persuading humans compared to other humans.

  • Personalized GPT-4 won debates 64.4% of the time
  • Effect persisted even when participants knew they were debating AI
  • Preregistered, randomized controlled trial (N = 900)

Salvi et al. (2025), Nature Human Behaviour, 9(8), 1645-1653

The BullshitBench Problem

Can AI detect its own nonsense? Mostly, no. BullshitBench v2 tested 70+ AI models with plausible-sounding but nonsensical prompts — including legal and financial scenarios.

  • Only Claude and Qwen score above 60% on bullshit detection
  • Chain-of-thought reasoning does not help
  • RLHF training exacerbates the problem

BullshitBench v2 & Liang et al. (2025), arXiv

What This Means for M&A

AI can produce compelling due diligence reports, persuasive pitchbooks, and sophisticated valuations — but without verification, they may contain confident errors. The role of human judgment in verifying AI output is becoming the single most critical professional skill.

The future isn't AI or humans. It's AI plus humans who know when not to trust the machine.
